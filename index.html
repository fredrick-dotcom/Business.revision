Paper 1 — Marketing & People

Covers Themes 1 & 2. 2h exam. Section A: short answer. Section B: data response. Section C: essay (20 marks). Calculators NOT permitted.

Topic Guide
Question Types
AI Practice

1-2 Mark Questions 1-2 marks

Identify / define questions. One sentence answers. No need to develop — just be precise.

  • Define the term "market share"
  • Identify one benefit of primary research
  • State the formula for contribution per unit

4 Mark Questions 4 marks

Explain questions. Make a point, explain it, apply to context. Two developed points needed.

  • Explain two advantages of niche marketing [4]
  • Explain why a business might use price skimming [4]

6 Mark Questions 6 marks

Analyse questions. Build a chain of reasoning. Apply to context throughout.

  • Analyse the impact of rising inflation on a retailer
  • Analyse why a business might use batch production

10 Mark Questions 10 marks

Evaluate questions. Two sides, chains of analysis, reach a justified conclusion. 3 band mark scheme.

  • Evaluate the best pricing strategy for a new luxury brand
  • Evaluate whether market research is essential for a startup

20 Mark Essay 20 marks

Section C discursive essay. Requires analysis, evaluation, judgement. KAA + Eval. Use examples.

  • To what extent is customer relationship management the most important driver of business success?
  • Evaluate the importance of the marketing mix for a growing business

Calculation Questions varies

Show all workings! Common calcs: break-even, contribution, profit margins, market share, PED/YED, labour productivity, capacity utilisation.

  • Calculate the break-even output
  • Calculate the gross profit margin
  • Calculate price elasticity of demand

Paper 2 — Business Activities

Covers Theme 3. 2h exam. Section A & B: data/context-based questions. Section C: 20-mark essay. Decision-making, strategy, finance.

Data Response 4-10 marks

Apply your knowledge to the case study/data given. Always reference figures and context.

  • Read ALL context carefully
  • Use data in your answer (quote figures)
  • Link analysis back to the business

Network Analysis/CPA 6-8 marks

Calculate EST, LFT, float. Identify critical path. Know how delays affect the project.

  • Complete nodes left-to-right for EST (take highest)
  • Right-to-left for LFT (take lowest)
  • Float = LFT − EST − Duration

Investment Appraisal 6-8 marks

Payback period, ARR, NPV. Show working. Compare options with analysis.

  • Payback: deduct net cash flows from investment year-by-year
  • ARR: (Total profit / Years) / Initial outlay × 100
  • NPV: Apply discount factors and sum present values

Decision Trees 6 marks

Calculate expected values and net gains. Select best option with justification.

  • Expected value = Financial outcome × Probability
  • Net gain = Total EV − Cost
  • Compare net gains of all options

20-Mark Essay 20 marks

Discursive evaluation. Balance of analysis and judgement. Reach a clear conclusion.

  • Use PEEL structure (Point, Evidence, Explain, Link)
  • Evaluate both sides
  • Contextualise to a relevant business

Ratio Analysis 4-6 marks

Calculate and interpret profitability, liquidity and efficiency ratios. Comment on trends.

  • Gross profit margin: GP/Revenue × 100
  • Current ratio: Current assets/Current liabilities
  • ROCE: Operating profit/Capital employed × 100

Paper 3 — Investigating Business

Covers Theme 4 + pre-release case study. 2h exam. All questions based on the pre-release context. Requires synthesis across all themes.

Topic Guide
Question Types
AI Practice
Pre-Release

Context-Based Analysis 6-10 marks

All questions relate to the pre-release scenario. Weave in facts from the case throughout.

  • Reference specific data from the pre-release material
  • Link global business concepts to the case
  • Consider stakeholder perspectives

Globalisation Questions 4-10 marks

Push/pull factors, trading blocs, FDI, emerging economies, MNC impacts.

  • Use BRICS/MINTS examples
  • Consider both host and home country effects
  • GDP, HDI, literacy, health indicators

Ethics & Stakeholders 10-20 marks

CSR, ethical vs profit motives, stakeholder conflicts. Weighted evaluation required.

  • Shareholders vs stakeholders
  • Short-term profit vs long-term reputation
  • Carroll's CSR pyramid

20-Mark Synoptic Essay 20 marks

Draws on all themes. Requires nuanced, multi-perspective evaluation. Strongest students reference the pre-release AND wider knowledge.

  • Use pre-release data as evidence
  • Cross-theme analysis (link to T1, T2, T3)
  • Justified, balanced conclusion
